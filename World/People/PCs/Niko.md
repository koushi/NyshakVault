### Background

1. When wearing it, you gain half it's HP (rounded up) + Int Mod as Temporary Hitpoints. These cannot be regained except by use of the Mend Spell or a Long Rest. When these Hit Points run out, the armor turns inert and remains so until a number of hours equal to the damage that exceeded the Temporary Hitpoints. So if you had 15 Temp Hp and took 20 damage, it's inert for five hours while it repairs itself. This time can be halved (or reduced by the number of hours worked on it) if you spend the waking hours of a long rest working on it. Or downtime. The Temp HP does stack with other similar effects as long as the effect says it can stack. You gain an additional set of arms that can do simple tasks, similar to a Thri-Kreen, that can also hold items. We can play with specifics, to sorta test a bit. See how it works. In regards to story, here's my thoughts and ideas on adjustments to make it fit the narrative a bit better. (just about everything is going to stay the same, because it works nicely already)
    
2. In the months leading up to the event that lead to Orvus' eventual death, he'd been getting visits from particular individuals. Your memories of them are fuzzy as it was before you'd become fully sentient. You could sense Orvus' growing unease. He was muttering in his sleep, tossing and turning. You could see the deep rings that began to form under his already aged eyes. It never diminished the kindness, but you could feel his pain, his fear. After the first visit by these dangerous strangers, Orvus seemed more interested in your growth. He worked with you, on you, respectfully. Always treated you like he cared. Gently and kindly. Your perceptions started to align. No longer a myriad of impulses and images seen from eyes that could feel. You took after the framework you were given, elongating yourself to Orvus' height and taking his shape. That of a humanoid. With shape came identity. You found the ember of curiosity, a once roaring fire now set ablaze anew by the fuel of possibility. The dangerous strangers weren't Orvus' only visitors of course. He was a bit of a hermit, and left home on seldom occasion but he had an agreement with the locals. They looked out for him, and he helped with repairs. His little asteroid was occasionally visited by kindly folk either requesting Orvus' expertise, or some manner of thank you in the form of food or supplies. An autognome from a nearby mining company was often brought through too to be serviced and repaired when damaged heavily. You were always curious to hear about his experiences. While he told them mechanically and without much pomp, it was always a fascinating experience. You could sense a fledgling sentience within that steely shell. The following days were some of the best you'd ever known. You realized in the night, voices touched your mind. You assumed them echoes of Orvus' lessons, bouncing through your mind repeatedly.
    

- These days were short-lived, and one morning in the early hours Niko 'woke' to see Orvus packing, putting anything and everything he could into bags and trunks. You, of course, helped him without even knowing why. He seemed to preoccupied to say, only saying 'I'll explain, we _must_ be away from here.' Climbing on a small skiff that was parked outside, a boat Niko had never seen before, they departed. Nike had never had the chance to see the system as a whole. Only glimpses from windows and the small plot of land outside of Orvus' workshop that he often used to pile supplies. Orvus immediately set about working on a device, muttering something about it being his only chance. You could see the tension in his body as he sat cross-legged amidst his pile of luggage in the small bunk room towards the nose of the ship, belowdecks. He had you hold it for a moment as he dug out some tools. The device seemed a simple ring of black iron with inexplicably floating motes of light within the ring. It almost looked like a little galaxy in the palm of your pseudopod. You could feel something pull at you, like it might disrupt your carefully kept surface tension and slurp your entire being into it. As you're handing it back to him, you hear yells from above and a massive spike crashes through the ceiling, slicing through you. You're stunned, hurt and suddenly a pool on the floor, and the device cracks as it hits the ground and something detonates. In a moment that seems to last an hour, you see a blossoming dark purple mass of energy swirl within the damaged device. You recognize the magicks as the manipulation of gravity. Something Orvus was an old hand at. But this was far more powerful than even Orvus could expect.
    

4. You saw the device erupt and before anything you felt your 'self' forcefully gripped and torn into the device. In a moment memories washed over you, many in third person as you saw old, rough hands work over any number of projects and a bloom of pride whenever the tired eyes swept over to the familiar figure of Niko. You could see your reflection in his surface. An old human with lazily-kept hair and simple clothing. And a moment later you were separated and you found yourself encompassing your creator, your mentor, your parent. The energies washed over you, changing...everything, even giving you your unique coloration. What you didn't realize though, was that you'd collected the device as well and when it erupted, it fused the three of you. Your body expanded with the force and crashed through the entire front of the ship, dislodging the invaders and throwing any on the deck into the merciless Astral Sea. It all happened so quickly, but you saw the attackers briefly before you shrunk back down. You saw as a Githyanki woman holding a Great Axe swung at your viciously while she floated away. You could see a teal marking on the back of one of her hands, though the exact symbol you couldn't see.
    

- Left among the destruction of a miniature black hole that was nearly contained, you wonder why you weren't also devoured. The attacking ship retreated quickly but the skiff you were on only had a small crew and it seemed that none remained. You floated for what felt like ages. Your pseudopods moved with skill that wasn't yours, and with a wisdom and knowledge that could have only belonged to Orvus himself, as you worked on this device that could no longer be removed from your person. Any attempts saw it fused and painful to your gelatinous form. You settled for letting it comprise a 'core' in the center of your chest. You also learned that gravity magic came easy to you now. You worked on the device, a single-minded attempt to get it fixed, even using the remains of the skiff and it's undamaged spelljamming helm to limp back to the asteroid you'd once called home. You defended your home several times, using the gravity magic to manipulate the density of your 'skin' to create an armor of sorts, a shell that you could ooze out of and leave to move on its own. Eventually the locals grew anxious of the destroyed ship docked there, and people stopped visiting. What felt like decades to Niko was actually weeks, as it would feel to someone whose first days sentient were filled with tragedy and loss. The incident was reported to local authorities. Finally, when Niko felt his resources were run through, he decided to take the limping ship to a nearby hub to search out answers. The device, he dubbed the Universal Magnet (that's a placeholder, we can change the name, you can even name it if you want), would not be fixed with the resources you have. The metal was too strong to be repaired by the means available to you. Seeking out someone skilled in the craft of unique metals and magical items, and to find answers about this group who had been harassing your caretaker. The Rock of Bral, a place Orvus mentioned often, was your first lead.
